The Case for Communism

Why Marxism?

01

The System Is the Problem

Nigeria has vast oil wealth, fertile land, and 200 million people. Yet the majority live in poverty. This is not bad leadership — it is capitalism. The profit motive guarantees wealth flows upward to the owning class. No election can fix a broken system. We need to change the system itself.

02

Workers Hold the Power

Who produces Nigeria's oil? Workers. Who builds Lagos? Workers. Who keeps hospitals, schools, and farms running? Workers. When workers act together — in unions, strikes, and organised political movements — they can shut down the entire economy. That power, organised, transforms society.

03

Marxism Is the Tool

Marxism is not a dogma — it is a scientific method for understanding how society works and how to change it. It explains why colonialism impoverished Africa, why austerity always falls on the poor, and why every capitalist politician from PDP to APC ends up serving the same ruling class.
